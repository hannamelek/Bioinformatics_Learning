## Alignment Analysis

### Fully Conserved Columns

Fully conserved columns contain the same amino acid in every sequence. These positions are highly conserved through evolution and are often essential for maintaining protein function or structure.

### Semi-Conserved Columns

Semi-conserved columns contain amino acids with similar chemical properties (for example, leucine, isoleucine, and valine). Although the residues differ, they often perform similar roles and preserve protein function.

### Variable Columns

Variable columns contain different amino acids among the aligned sequences. These regions usually represent evolutionary divergence and may contribute to species-specific adaptations.

## Biological Significance

Highly conserved residues often correspond to:

- Enzyme active sites
- Ligand or substrate binding sites
- Structural core of the protein
- Functionally important amino acids

Mutations in these regions are more likely to affect protein function because they have been preserved throughout evolution.
